Pierre Calmels

My name is Pierre. I was born in New Milford, Connecticut" on 11/11/1980. I like to hike, walk on empty beaches, and read fiction. I also like works in history, and some philosophy. I have an ever growing library, and an ever growing list of books I'd like to read. I hope to live long enough so I can get through most of them. My employment history consists of some varied and interesting experinces. My most gainful employment thus far has been as a bartender, working at some great places including; The Pequot Inn, The Mayflower Inn, The Roger Sherman Inn, The Cobb's Mill Inn, and Le Chateau Restaurant. My experiences at these places were formative and lasting. People have asked me for stories, but part of the bartending job is never disclosing too much. I'd choose serving a cat, water, from a monkey dish at the downstairs bar of Harry's New York Bar, Paris as the high point of my restaurant career! It was downhill from there. I also had temporary roles in the Legal Department of UBS Investment Bank, and also in their Wealth Management Unit, and once took seasonal employment as a package loader at UPS. One of my more interesting gigs was a summer job working for a movie catering company on such films as; Old Dogs, College Road Trip, and Indiana Jones and the Cystal Skull. One thing I learned from each of these jobs is that some of the lowest positions offer the best perspectives. If you aren't important, you will often learn about things well above your pay grade, and usually, no one will care. So, maybe I have gotten to see relatively little, because a bar room is pretty small. But I also saw somethings in great detail, some strange, some scary, some great. albeit without many experiences from outside to contrast from. Thankfully though, along the way I got degrees from Naugatuck Valley Community College and Ball State University -- GO Cardinals! For the past three years while finishing my Bachelor's I have been driving an Uber through-out Connecticut. Out of necessity I learned how to fix my car, mostly from You Tube videos and then getting my hands and everything else, dirty. And in the past two months I have started learning to code, one of the most challenging things I have ever done, and I hope it will also be one of the most rewarding.
